3. Specialized Areas of Security

‚ñº

Five Key Areas:

  • Physical Security: Protection of physical assets and facilities
  • Operations Security: Protection of operational processes and procedures
  • Communications Security: Protection of communication channels and data in transit
  • Cyber Security: Protection against cyber threats and attacks
  • Network Security: Protection of network infrastructure and data flows

4. Components of Information Security

‚ñº

CIA Triad

üîí Confidentiality

Information is accessible only to authorized individuals

üõ°Ô∏è Integrity

Information is accurate, complete, and unaltered

‚ö° Availability

Information is accessible when needed by authorized users

CNSS Model (McCumber Cube)

A comprehensive model that considers information in three states (storage, processing, transmission) across the three CIA dimensions and three countermeasure categories (policy, technology, education).

5. Supporting Concepts

‚ñº

Key Supporting Concepts:

  • Privacy: The right to be left alone and control personal information
  • Identification: Claiming an identity (username)
  • Authentication: Proving the claimed identity (password)
  • Authorization: Granting access rights based on verified identity
  • Accountability: Tracking and recording actions for responsibility

6. Key InfoSec Concepts

‚ñº

Essential Definitions:

  • Threats: Potential dangers that could harm assets
  • Threat Agents: Entities that carry out threats
  • Attacks: Deliberate actions against assets
  • Exploits: Techniques used to take advantage of vulnerabilities
  • Vulnerabilities: Weaknesses that could be exploited
